What is this?

A directory of every launched Y Combinator startup, rewritten without jargon. Every description is in plain words; each profile keeps the company's original wording underneath, so you can always see what they actually say. It's for anyone who reads "synergistic B2B SaaS platform" and feels nothing.

What is Y Combinator?

Y Combinator (YC) is the best-known startup investor and school. A few times a year it picks a group of new companies, gives each some money in exchange for a small ownership stake, and coaches them for about three months. Airbnb, Stripe, Dropbox, DoorDash and Reddit all started there.

What is a batch?

Each group that goes through YC together is a batch, named after its season and year and shown here as a short code: W26 is Winter 2026, X25 is Spring 2025, S25 is Summer 2025, F24 is Fall 2024.

The categories

B2B — sells to other companies. Consumer — sells to regular people. Fintech — money. Healthcare — fixing humans. Industrials — machines, energy, space. Real Estate & Construction. Education. Government — selling to or fixing public systems.

The signals

Every profile shows the four things investors look at, each rated high, medium or low. For about fifty-five well-known companies these are hand-written; for everyone else they are read automatically from the data (status, stage, team size, category) and labeled as such.

  • Product signal — do people actually use and love it?
  • Founder signal — are the founders unusually determined or experienced?
  • Competition — who else is after the same thing, and can this company still win?
  • Market opportunity — how big is the prize if it works?

Data

Names, batches, categories and descriptions come from the public yc-oss API, a daily-updated mirror of the official YC directory (snapshot: July 2026). Founder names, titles, photos and social links come from each company's public YC page. Stories and signals for about fifty-five well-known companies were written by hand.

The automatic caveman translations are simple word substitution — occasionally they come out strange. That is part of the charm. For exact wording, see the original description on any profile.
